Classic Filipino Cuisines

Adobo

Adobo, a beloved Filipino dish, brims with rich, savory flavors. Marinated in a blend of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and spices, meats like chicken or pork absorb the enticing concoction. Simmered to perfection, the tender meat becomes a vessel for the infusion of delectable aromas, resulting in a tangy, slightly sweet taste. Adobo, often paired with steamed rice, stands as a staple in Filipino cuisine, cherished for its irresistible appeal and cultural significance.

Tinola

Tinola is a comforting Filipino soup known for its light and refreshing flavors. Traditionally made with chicken, the soup features a clear broth infused with ginger, garlic, and fish sauce. The broth is simmered with tender chicken pieces, green papaya or chayote, and fragrant chili leaves. Tinola is cherished for its soothing warmth and delicate balance of savory and herbal notes. It is often enjoyed as a nourishing and comforting meal, particularly during cooler weather or as a remedy for colds and illnesses.

Caldereta

Caldereta is a mouthwatering Filipino dish renowned for its bold and robust flavors. Typically made with tender beef, the meat is simmered in a rich tomato-based sauce infused with onions, garlic, bell peppers, and a medley of aromatic spices. Potatoes and carrots are often added to enhance the dish's heartiness. The result is a tantalizing combination of savory, tangy, and slightly spicy notes that make Caldereta a beloved centerpiece of Filipino feasts and celebrations.

Kare Kare

Kare-Kare is a delectable Filipino dish that captures the essence of Filipino cuisine. This rich and hearty stew features tender oxtail, beef, or tripe cooked in a thick, savory peanut sauce. Enhanced with a medley of vegetables like eggplant, bok choy, and string beans, Kare-Kare delivers a delightful blend of flavors and textures. Often served with shrimp paste on the side and enjoyed with steamed rice, Kare-Kare is a beloved Filipino classic, renowned for its unique taste and culinary artistry.

Papaitan

Papaitan is a bold and flavorful Filipino dish that tantalizes the taste buds. This hearty soup is traditionally made with goat or beef innards, such as liver, intestines, and stomach, simmered in a savory broth infused with bitter flavors. Enhanced with ginger, garlic, onions, and spices, Papaitan delivers a unique combination of rich, tangy, and slightly bitter notes. Loved for its robust taste and cultural significance, Papaitan is a testament to the diverse and adventurous nature of Filipino cuisine.

Sisig

Sisig is a sizzling and savory delight that embodies the spirit of Filipino cuisine. This iconic dish is typically made from chopped pig's face and ears, seasoned with tangy calamansi juice, soy sauce, and aromatic spices. Served on a hot plate, the sisig is cooked to perfection, achieving a delightful crispy texture. With its bold flavors and mouthwatering aroma, Sisig has gained popularity for its unique blend of sour, salty, and umami tastes. A true Filipino favorite, Sisig is a must-try for food enthusiasts seeking a delicious and unforgettable culinary experience.
